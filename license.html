DRIVING LICENSE

Driving license

In India, a driving license is an official document that authorises its holder to operate various types of motor vehicleson highways and some other roads to which the public has access. In various Indian states, they are administered by the Regional Transport Authorities/Offices (RTA/RTO). A driving license is required in India by any person driving a vehicle on any highway or other road defined in the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. This act sets limits on the minimum age for vehicle operation ranging from 16 to 20, depending on specific circumstances.

Eligibility for Driving license in India :-

To receive a permanent DL in India, one must fulfil the following eligibility criteria.

  • The applicant must be at least 18 years old.
  • The applicant must first obtain a Learner's license and apply for DL after 30 days.
  • The applicant needs to submit his/her DL application within 6 months of LL issuance.
  • The applicant has to successfully pass the tests associated with LL and DL.


Documents Required

Documents required for driving license?

You need the following records to apply for a driving license in India:

  • Your age proof (Submit one of the following documents)
  • Birth Certificate
  • Passport
  • PAN Card
  • 10th class mark sheet
  • Transfer certificate with your date of birth printed on it.
  • Your current address proof (If you are applying for a license in a state other than your home state)
  • Rental agreement and electricity bill
  • Your permanent address proof (Submit one of the following)
  • Passport
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Electricity bill (issued in your name)
  • Ration Card
  • LIC bond
  • Voters ID Card

    • Additional requirements for the application process :-

  • Application form

  • 1 passport-sized photograph for the application for a permanent license
  • Application Fees
  • If you are above the age of 40, you also need to submit a medical certificate - Form 1 and 1A that a government doctor issue.

Steps for Applying -

Driving license application process in India -

There are both online and offline DL application options available in India. It allows applicants to choose a preferred method of getting a DL issued. The following sections highlight the processes for online and offline applications for a new Driving license in India.

How to apply for a Driving license online in India -

Here is how you can apply for a Driving license online in India.

  • Step 1: Visit the official transport website known as Parivahan Sewa.
  • Step 2: Upon reaching the home screen, get to the “Online Services” and then to "Driving license Related Services".
  • Step 3: You will be redirected to another portal called Sarathi.
  • Step 4: Select the state (location) where you live and then choose “Apply for Learner's license (LL)”.
  • Step 5: Submit your LL application along with appropriate documents and the required fee.
  • Step 6: Take your preliminary exam on the scheduled date of the test to obtain an LL.
  • Step 7: Hold on to the LL for at least 30 days and keep practising your driving skills.
  • Step 8: Now, get to the Sarathi portal once again to apply for a permanent Driving license.
  • Step 9: Via the “Apply for Driving license” section, you can fill in your application as an applicant holding LL.
  • Step 10: Get a driving test slot, pay the charges and get your receipt.
  • Step 11: Visit the local Regional Transport Office (RTO) to take your DL test.

An officer will conduct and assess your performance during the driving test. Then, you will be sent to record your biometric data. After that, you can wait for the DL to be delivered to your address through the postal service.



How to apply for a Driving license offline in India -

Here is how you can apply for a Driving license offline in India -

  • Step 1: Visit the Parivahan platform to download Form 2 or obtain it at your local Regional Transport Office.
  • Step 2: Fill out details in Form 2 for Learning license and submit at the local RTO with other documents.
  • Step 3: You need to choose a slot for the Learning license test and an application number.
  • Step 4: Use the application number to appear for the test and receive your LL upon successful completion.
  • Step 5: Select a slot for your DL driving test as per the given options.
  • Step 6: Submit the standard application fee and get your acknowledgement slip.
  • Step 7: Visit the RTO on the scheduled date to take your test.

Upon completing the test, you will be requested to record your biometric details. Then, the RTO will take a couple of days for document verification before sending your DL through courier/post.



Types of Driving licenses in India -

The types of Driving licenses differ based on the type of vehicle they are issued for in India. Here are the five main categories of DLs one can apply for -

  • Gearless two-wheeler DL (Such as mopeds and gearless scooters)
  • Geared two-wheeler DL (Such as bikes and geared scooters)
  • Light motor vehicle DL (Such as sedans, hatchbacks, SUVs, etc.)
  • Transport vehicle DL (Such as trucks, vans, buses, etc.)
  • Other categories (Such as a vehicle that transports hazardous items)
